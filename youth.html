Planting Possibility in Young Hands

  • Where kids dig into the soil and discover their own potential.

Growing the Next Generation

Our Youth Gardening & Education program gives children and teens the opportunity to explore nature, learn how food is grown, and discover the value of teamwork — all in a hands-on garden environment. Through seasonal workshops, school partnerships, and community-led activities, we help young people connect with the earth and each other.

We believe that the garden is more than a place to plant — it's a place to grow. By connecting kids to the land and to each other, we nurture confidence, curiosity, and care for the environment. These early experiences shape not only healthier habits, but a stronger sense of community belonging.

Sharing the Harvest, Sharing the Love

The Harvest Sharing program ensures that the food we grow is shared with those who need it most. A portion of each harvest is donated to local food banks, schools, and families experiencing food insecurity — turning every garden bed into a source of hope and nourishment.

This program is rooted in the belief that abundance is meant to be shared. When we grow together and give together, we not only feed bodies — we feed connection, compassion, and community resilience.

What Youth Can Expect in the Garden

Our youth program is designed to spark curiosity, build confidence, and connect kids with the earth — one seed at a time. Whether it's their first time in the garden or they're already little green thumbs, children and teens will find support, joy, and hands-on learning in every visit.

  • Age-appropriate garden beds and tools for kids
  • Fun, seasonal planting and harvesting activities
  • Garden mentors and educators to guide learning
  • Workshops on planting, harvesting, and soil health
  • A safe, inclusive space for discovery and connection
